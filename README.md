# 🦉 The Memory Thief: Quest for Lost Knowledge

> A fantasy adventure game that teaches AI's **Three Retrieval Modes** through exploration, puzzles, and storytelling—not quizzes.

## 🎮 About the Game

**The Memory Thief** is an interactive browser adventure inspired by the **AI Prompting in 2026** course.

A mysterious villain known as **The Memory Thief** has stolen the kingdom's Knowledge Shards, causing books to become blank, maps to disappear, and people to forget important information.

As the newest apprentice of the Grand Library, your mission is to explore the kingdom, recover the stolen knowledge, help its citizens, and uncover the truth behind the thief.

Unlike traditional educational games, players are never asked multiple-choice questions or quizzes. Instead, the core AI concept is learned naturally through gameplay.

---

## 🎯 Educational Concept

This game teaches **Concept 3 – The Three Retrieval Modes** from the *AI Prompting in 2026* course.

Players gradually discover that different situations require different ways of finding information:

* 📚 **Memory Pulse** — Uses existing knowledge already available.
* 🔭 **Sky Scan** — Finds fresh and up-to-date information.
* 🦉 **Owl Scouts** — Performs deeper investigations for complex problems.

Rather than explaining these concepts directly, the game lets players experience their strengths and limitations through exploration and problem-solving.

---

## 🌍 Gameplay

Explore a colorful fantasy kingdom filled with secrets and adventure.

Features include:

* 🗺️ Explore multiple locations
* 🧩 Environmental puzzles
* 📖 Story-driven quests
* 🦉 Memorable NPCs
* 💎 Hidden Knowledge Shards
* 🎒 Inventory system
* ⭐ Character progression
* 🏆 Achievements
* 🎵 Sound effects and animations
* 💾 Local save system

Every challenge encourages players to think about **how** knowledge is found instead of simply choosing the correct answer.

---

## 🎓 Learning Through Play

The goal of this project was to move beyond the traditional educational quiz.

Instead of asking:

> "Which retrieval mode should you use?"

The game creates situations where players naturally discover:

* Sometimes knowledge already exists.
* Sometimes information must be current.
* Sometimes a deeper investigation is required.

Learning happens through the consequences of exploration, experimentation, and discovery.
